Generative AI-Driven Chatbots
for Educational Support

This research presents the development of a suite of four Generative AI chatbots, each tailored to module-specific subject areas: Programming, Mathematics, English, and Technical Subjects. These chatbots are designed to address the critical gap in current educational technologies by fostering deep conceptual understanding through step-by-step guidance.

Project Scope

Comprehensive development of AI-driven educational chatbots targeting specific learning modules

Literature Survey

The integration of Artificial Intelligence in education has led to a paradigm shift in teaching and learning methodologies. Traditional education systems have long relied on instructor-led teaching, static curricula, and standardized assessments, which often fail to accommodate individual learning styles and needs.

  • Evolution from rule-based expert systems to adaptive ML and deep learning techniques
  • Natural Language Processing revolutionizing automated tutoring
  • AI chatbots promoting active learning strategies and critical thinking

Research Gap

Current educational technologies often focus on providing direct answers rather than fostering deep conceptual understanding. There is a critical gap in systems that provide step-by-step guidance while enhancing critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Current Limitations

  • • Direct answer provision without explanation
  • • Lack of module-specific customization
  • • Limited step-by-step guidance

Our Solution

  • • Socratic questioning methodology
  • • Subject-specific AI architectures
  • • Progressive learning approach

Research Problem & Solution

Problem Statement

Students lack access to personalized, module-specific tutoring that promotes conceptual understanding rather than memorization.

Proposed Solution

Development of four specialized AI chatbots using distinct methodologies: SLMs for programming, LLMs for mathematics and English, and RAG for technical subjects.

Research Objectives

1

Develop scalable and cost-effective GenAI chatbots for university curricula

2

Implement distinct AI methodologies for different subject areas

3

Enhance critical thinking and problem-solving skills through step-by-step guidance

4

Evaluate performance across accuracy, clarity, and engagement dimensions

Methodology

Programming

Small Language Models (SLMs) with Socratic questioning

Mathematics

Large Language Models (LLMs) with symbolic computation

English

LLMs with audio-visual interaction capabilities

Technical

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) approach

Technologies Used

AI Models

  • • DeepSeek, Phi-3, CodeBERT
  • • Meta-Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
  • • Gemma LLM
  • • Mathewhe/DCLM-7B-Chat

Frameworks

  • • ReactJS (Frontend)
  • • FastAPI (Backend)
  • • Flask (English Module)
  • • Langchain (Embeddings)

Infrastructure

  • • MongoDB (Database)
  • • AWS EC2 (Deployment)
  • • Docker (Containerization)
  • • Ollama v0.5.14
